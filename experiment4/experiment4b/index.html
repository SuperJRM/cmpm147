Experiment 4B - Infinite Worlds

  • Experiment 4A

Description

Description: One of two generators, the worlds they generate are meant to mimic that of old Pokemon games, specifically Gold and Silver. This world uses a bright color palette, similar to those used to counter a dim Gameboy screen, to bring a spring, and maybe even winter, scene to life. --Codes:-- _ places down ground tiles, : places trees, and w creates tiles of water. --Life:-- In this world, streams and ponds move gently during the spring, with winter bringing the fall of powdered snow. (using tiles from [Micro Tileset - Overworld & Dungeon](https://thkaspar.itch.io/micro-tileset-overworld-dungeon) by [thkaspar](https://thkaspar.itch.io/))

Technical

Autotiling was used to place down appropriate sprites, with noise randomizing which tiles were used. The water and snow use time passed to change sprites per second passed, mimicking animation.

Reflection

Working on the project alone, I found building the generators to be a very fun experience. Having grown up with sprite based games, I thought it was really cool to actually make some kind of homage to them in a way. For that reason, I feel that the highs of the project definitely came from seeing everything in action. However, there were a lot of lows that came from technical issues. On top of the usual slew of bugs, I also had to deal with the constant crashing of Glitch during certain periods, where the site would break whenever any changes were made to the code. I also had an incredibly difficult time with fitting both generators into one portfolio page, to the point where I eventually gave up and just split the two. Overall, though, I am glad I was able to create something I am proud of, and that I was able to learn so much about autotiling!
